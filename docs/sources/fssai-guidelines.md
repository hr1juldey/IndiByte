# FSSAI Guidelines for Food Composition in India

## Overview

The Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) sets comprehensive regulations for food safety, composition, and labeling in India. These guidelines are essential for ensuring food safety, nutritional labeling accuracy, and consumer protection.

## Key Requirements (2025 Regulations)

- All food packaging must prominently feature nutritional information
- Mandatory allergen warnings on packaging
- Clear symbols indicating vegetarian or non-vegetarian status
- Expiration dates must be clearly marked
- FSSAI license numbers required on all products
- Hygienic handling procedures for delivery staff in online food enterprises

## Significance for IndiByte

FSSAI guidelines provide regulatory framework that ensures nutritional data accuracy and food safety. For IndiByte's mission to standardize nutrition for India's culinary diversity, these guidelines serve as an important reference for:

- Nutritional labeling standards
- Food safety requirements
- Regulatory compliance for food businesses
- Consumer safety measures

## Food Business Compliance

Food businesses must comply with FSSAI regulations which include:

- Proper nutritional information display
- Allergen declarations
- Expiration date labeling
- License number display
- Hygienic handling practices

## Recent Developments

- Proposed ban on PFAs and BPA in food packaging materials
- New directive on removal of 'ORS' from product labels and brand names
- Amendments to Foods for Infant Nutrition Regulations 2020
- "Eat Right Thali of India" initiative for public education
