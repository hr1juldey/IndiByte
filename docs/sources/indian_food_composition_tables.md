# Indian Food Composition Tables (IFCT)

## Overview
The Indian Food Composition Tables (IFCT) is a comprehensive compilation of nutritional information for 528 key Indian foods. It serves as a critical resource for researchers, academics, nutritionists, and health professionals working with Indian dietary patterns and nutrition analysis.

## Key Details
- **Number of foods**: 528 key Indian foods
- **Primary use**: Nutritional analysis and research
- **Maintained by**: Indian Council of Medical Research - National Institute of Nutrition (ICMR-NIN)
- **Purpose**: Standard reference for nutritional content of Indian foods
- **Applications**: Diet assessment, nutritional counseling, food labeling, and research studies

## Significance for IndiByte API
The IFCT serves as a foundational resource for developing comprehensive Indian food nutrition databases like the one described in the IndiByte API. It provides:
- Standardized nutritional values for traditional Indian foods
- Reliable data for dietary analysis and recommendations
- Baseline information for developing culturally appropriate dietary guidelines
- Reference values for validating nutrition content in digital platforms

## Usage Context
The IFCT is used widely by:
- Researchers conducting nutritional studies in India
- Academics teaching nutrition and dietetics
- Government agencies developing dietary guidelines
- Healthcare professionals providing nutrition counseling
- Food industry for product development and labeling

## Relationship to Modern Applications
Modern applications like the IndiByte API leverage foundational databases like IFCT to provide:
- Accurate nutritional information for traditional Indian foods
- Cultural context for dietary recommendations
- Medical condition-specific dietary guidance
- Regional food variations and preferences
