# Peer-Reviewed Research Papers on Indian Food Nutritional Composition

## Overview

Peer-reviewed research forms the scientific foundation for accurate nutritional data. For Indian foods specifically, numerous research papers provide evidence-based nutritional information that supports databases like IndiByte.

## Key Research Areas

- Nutritional composition of traditional Indian foods
- Regional variations in food composition
- Impact of cooking methods on nutritional value
- Bioavailability of nutrients in Indian food preparations
- Dietary patterns and health outcomes in Indian populations

## Notable Research Studies

### ICMR-INDIAB Study

- The ICMR-INDIAB survey provides comprehensive data on dietary profiles and associated metabolic risk factors in India
- Large-scale population-based study covering multiple states
- Includes detailed dietary intake patterns and nutritional analysis
- Provides evidence for dietary guidelines and food composition data

### Millet Research

- Studies on millets like Kodo millet and their functional protein quality
- Nutritional analysis of traditional Indian grains
- Health benefits and bioactive compounds in Indian foods
- Acceptability and nutritional composition of millet-based products

### Traditional Food Systems

- Research on traditional food preparation methods
- Nutritional value of indigenous foods
- Impact of traditional processing on nutrient retention
- Cultural food practices and nutritional outcomes

## Significance for IndiByte

Peer-reviewed research provides:

- Scientific validation for nutritional values
- Evidence-based health claims
- Methodology for nutrient analysis
- Cross-validation of data from multiple sources
- Updates on new findings in Indian food nutrition

## Research Databases and Journals

## Quality Standards

- Peer review process ensures scientific rigor
- Reproducible methodology for data collection
- Standardized analytical techniques
- Statistical validation of results
- Cross-referencing with international databases
