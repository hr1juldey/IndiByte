# ICMR-INDIAB Survey and Indian Dietary Patterns Research

## Overview

The ICMR-INDIAB (Indian Council of Medical Research-India Diabetes) survey is a comprehensive, nationally representative study that provides critical insights into dietary patterns, nutritional status, and metabolic health in India. This document summarizes the key findings and implications of this research for the IndiByte project.

## ICMR-INDIAB Survey Overview

### Study Design and Scope

- Nationally representative cross-sectional study
- Focus on diabetes and related metabolic disorders
- Covers both urban and rural populations across India
- Includes comprehensive dietary assessment components
- Multi-state sampling methodology

### Key Research Objectives

- Assess prevalence of diabetes and pre-diabetes
- Evaluate dietary patterns across different regions
- Analyze metabolic risk factors
- Document nutritional transitions in Indian populations
- Understand regional dietary variations

## Key Findings on Dietary Patterns

### Carbohydrate Dominance

The ICMR-INDIAB research reveals concerning dietary patterns:

- 62% of daily calories come from refined carbohydrates (rice, wheat, sugar)
- Rice dominates in South, East, and Northeast India
- Wheat consumption is prevalent in North and Central India
- Millets remain important in select states

### Protein Deficiency

- Significantly lower protein intake than recommended levels
- Regional variations in protein sources and consumption
- Plant-based protein underutilization
- Economic barriers to animal protein consumption

### Regional Variations

The survey documents significant regional differences:

- Southern states: Higher rice consumption, fish as primary protein
- Northern states: Wheat-based diets with dairy and legume proteins
- Eastern states: Rice-fish dietary patterns with seasonal vegetables
- Western states: Diverse patterns with millets and legumes
- Northeastern states: Unique dietary patterns with local ingredients
