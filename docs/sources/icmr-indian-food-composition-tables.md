# ICMR Indian Food Composition Tables (IFCT) 2017

## Overview

The Indian Food Composition Tables (IFCT) 2017 is a comprehensive compilation of nutritional information for 528 key Indian foods, published by the Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) - National Institute of Nutrition (NIN). This database serves as a crucial resource for researchers, academics, policymakers, and regulators across India and some South Asian countries.

## Key Details

- **Publisher**: Indian Council of Medical Research - National Institute of Nutrition (ICMR-NIN)
- **Publication Year**: 2017
- **Number of Foods**: 528 key Indian foods
- **Purpose**: Provide standardized nutritional information for Indian foods
- **Usage**: Widely used by researchers, academics, policymakers, and regulators

## Content Coverage

The IFCT includes nutritional information for various categories of Indian foods:

- Cereals and millets
- Pulses and legumes
- Vegetables and fruits
- Spices and condiments
- Animal source foods
- Traditional preparations

## Significance for IndiByte

The IFCT 2017 serves as a foundational data source for IndiByte's nutritional database. It provides the standardized nutritional values that IndiByte uses to validate its food composition data. This ensures that IndiByte's recommendations are based on scientifically validated information recognized by ICMR, which is essential for the medical accuracy mentioned in the FAQ.

## Access and Usage

The IFCT is used as a reference standard for:

- Nutritional analysis of Indian diets
- Dietary assessment in research studies
- Policy formulation related to nutrition
- Development of nutrition education materials
- Clinical and public health applications

## Research Applications

The IFCT has been cited in numerous research studies and surveys, including the ICMR-INDIAB study, which examines dietary patterns and metabolic risk factors across India. It serves as a critical tool for understanding India's diverse food patterns and their nutritional implications.
