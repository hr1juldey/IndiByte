# Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI): Food Safety and Nutrition Standards

## Overview

The Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) is the apex body responsible for regulating and supervising food safety and standards in India. Established under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006, FSSAI plays a critical role in ensuring that food products in India meet safety and nutritional standards.

## Key Responsibilities

- **Food Safety Regulation**: Establishing and enforcing food safety standards across the food supply chain
- **Nutrition Policy**: Providing scientific advice and technical support on food safety and nutrition policy
- **Labeling Requirements**: Mandating labeling requirements for food products to ensure consumer awareness
- **Data Collection**: Gathering and maintaining food safety and nutrition data
- **Quality Assurance**: Ensuring that food products meet established safety and quality parameters

## Food Safety Standards

FSSAI sets comprehensive standards for:

- Food additives and preservatives
- Contaminant limits in food products
- Microbiological safety requirements
- Hygiene and sanitation standards
- Packaging and labeling regulations

## Nutrition-Related Functions

- **Nutritional Labeling**: Requiring accurate nutritional information on food packaging
- **Fortification Standards**: Establishing standards for food fortification programs
- **Health Claims Regulation**: Regulating health and nutrition claims made on food products
- **Dietary Guidelines**: Contributing to national dietary guidelines and nutrition recommendations

## Technology and Data Systems

FSSAI utilizes various technology platforms for:

- **Data Collection**: Gathering information on food safety and nutritional parameters
- **Mobile Testing Labs**: Deploying mobile food testing laboratories for on-site analysis
- **Digital Compliance**: Implementing digital systems for food business operator compliance
- **Public Awareness**: Creating awareness campaigns on food safety and nutrition
